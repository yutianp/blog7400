Pluspy
Pluspy is a low budget 1987 film that many credit with launching the popular drifting scene in Japan, from there it caught on in the USA and further afield, and today it’s one of the fastest growing forms of motorsport in the world. The concept of power-sliding a car through corners was far from new in the 1980s, but as a form of motorsport in and of itself it was new – and it’s widely believed that it was invented by Kunimitsu Takahashi of Japan. Takahashi’s most famous disciple is Keiichi Tsuchiya – a driver with wins and significant performances in various motorsport disciples from Le Mans endurance driving to NASCAR, the Japanese Formula Three Championship, and the Japanese Touring Car Championship. In ’87 the legend of Tsuchiya’s street racing style had grown to the point that a number of popular Japanese car magazines and tuning garages agreed to produce a video of Tsuchiya’s drifting skills on a mountain pass.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=blFur7piEnI&t=124s

Blender Map
To recreate the maps I had to find the exact location of this stage was quite troublesome as in the game the location states that it is in New England however, it turns out it was in Washington. Using the website AllTrails which allows users to create and share their own routes, I plotted down all the points of the stage where I then converted the trail into a GPX file which includes the elevation which I then importedd into Blender to create a 3D visualisation of the stage.

SHAKEDOWN.otf
I began creating a typeface to use for my pacenotes that I plan to overlay on top of my website and experimented with different styles with the thought of the notes themselves in mind. I wanted to create something that was similar to timekeeping and ended up with an LCD stopwatch styled typeface. I found it particularly interesting when trying to marry written text and the actual symbols used in pacenotes into existing glyphs and symbols used in computers.
